# Institute of Digital Risk (IDR) – Assignment

This project is a frontend design and development assignment for creating the brand and homepage of the **Institute of Digital Risk (IDR)**.  
The goal was to design a clean, responsive website and a minimal logo that represents structure, resilience, and digital risk management.

# Project Overview

The **Institute of Digital Risk (IDR)** is an industry-led training and deployment institute focused on preparing professionals for roles in digital, cyber, and AI risk.

This project includes:

- Logo design
- Responsive homepage
- Service model presentation
- Community and contact section

The design follows a **minimal, modern tech/education style**.

---

# Logo Design

The IDR logo uses a **cube-inspired geometric icon** to represent:

- Structure
- Stability
- Multiple layers of digital risk

### Color Palette
- **Orange** – innovation, energy, technology
- **Black** – professionalism and trust
- **White** – clarity and simplicity

The logo works in two variants:

- Icon only
- Icon + text (“Institute of Digital Risk”)

The design is kept minimal to ensure it remains clear at **small sizes such as favicons or mobile headers**.

# Website Sections

## 1. Hero Section
Introduces IDR’s mission with a strong headline and call-to-action.

- Mission headline
- Short description
- CTA button

---

## 2. About IDR
Explains the purpose of the institute as an **industry-led training and deployment organisation** focused on digital and cyber risk roles in regulated environments.

It highlights collaboration between:
- Academic research
- Industry practice

---

## 3. Service Pillars

The website presents IDR’s core model through three pillars:

### Academy
Training programs and bootcamps for students and professionals in digital risk and cybersecurity.

### Innovation & Incubation
Developing new frameworks, AI governance models, and future risk management solutions.

### Advisory Services
Helping organisations implement security and risk frameworks such as:

- NIST
- ISO 27001
- NIS2

---

## 4. Community

IDR serves:

- Students
- Early-career professionals
- Industry practitioners

Particularly those working in sectors such as:

- Financial services
- Critical infrastructure
- Regulated industries

---

## 5. Contact Section

- Name
